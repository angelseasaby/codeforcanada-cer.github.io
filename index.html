Call for Participants

Hello, bonjour! Code for Canada, in collaboration with the National Energy Board, are looking for participants to give us feedback and join workshops which will explore uses of a regulatory documents database, REGDOCS. Each workshop will take approximately 1 hour. You do not need to be familiar with this database to participate, we welcome anyone within Canada to apply!

These workshops will guide direction for increasing accessibility, discoverability, and participation within a publicly available database so that all residents, including landowners, scientists, and Indigenous communities, can fully participate in the national energy dialogue.

Workshops will be conducted remotely and in person based on your preferred method of communication. We are also seeking participants to test ideas. Please indicate what you prefer below, by clicking "I want to participate".

Compensation will be provided. The deadline to apply is September 16, 2019 at 1:00pm.

Code for Canada is a national nonprofit that connects government innovators with the tech and design community. Our programs enable governments to deliver better digital public services and empower communities to solve civic challenges using technology and design.

The National Energy Board is committed to protecting the privacy rights of individuals.

The definition of “personal information” is any information, in any form, about an identifiable individual. Refer to section 3 of the Privacy Act for further details regarding personal information.

Provision of your information is voluntary, and will not be used to make any administrative decisions about you.

Personal information is collected and used as described in the Treasury Board Secretariat’s Standard Personal Information Bank Outreach Activities (PSU 938)

Individuals have the right to file a complaint with the Privacy Commissioner of Canada regarding the department’s handling of their personal information.
